Short bio

Justice Cow is the project of multi-instrumentalist and songwriter Jessica Kion — indie folk for fans of Wednesday, Big Thief, and Sufjan Stevens. Based in New England with roots at Berklee and a decade of touring across the US and abroad, Kion writes unflinching songs about grief, memory, and community, performed solo, as a duo, or with a full band. She teaches songwriting at Brown University and is a frequent collaborator across the New England music scene.

Bio

Justice Cow is the project of Jessica Kion — a multi-instrumentalist, songwriter, and multidisciplinary artist who grew up in a musical family and studied at Berklee College of Music, shaped early on by artists like Regina Spektor and Sufjan Stevens. Over the past decade she has toured extensively across the US and abroad, equally at home in living rooms, listening clubs, and festival stages.

Her debut full-length, my dad died, is an unflinching record about grief, memory, and the moments that come flooding back. Written by Kion and recorded over four days at Big Nice Studio in Lincoln, RI with producer Bradford Krieger, it pairs intimate songwriting with full-band arrangements — vocals and acoustic guitar by Kion, saxophone and harmonies from Tyler Brasch, electric guitar and synths from Ben Levin, drums/banjo/bass from Krieger, additional harmonies from Caitlyn O'Brien, and mastering by Randy Roos at Squam Sound Studio.

A deeply community-minded artist, Kion is a frequent collaborator across the New England scene — performing and recording with Wrecking Glass, Tyler Brasch, Ben Levin, Bradford Krieger, and many others — and a regular presence at festivals, workshops, and house shows. She also teaches songwriting at Brown University, where she works with students on turning personal experience into finished songs.

Kion has just finished tracking her next full-length album, with a release planned for later this year.
