Bio

Justice Cow is the project of Jessica Kion — a multi-instrumentalist and songwriter who grew up in a musical family and studied at Berklee College of Music, shaped early on by artists like Regina Spektor and Sufjan Stevens.

Her debut full-length, my dad died, is an unflinching record about grief, memory, and the moments that come flooding back. Written by Kion and recorded over four days at Big Nice Studio in Lincoln, RI with producer Bradford Krieger, it pairs intimate songwriting with full-band arrangements — vocals and acoustic guitar by Kion, saxophone and harmonies from Tyler Brasch, electric guitar and synths from Ben Levin, drums/banjo/bass from Krieger, additional harmonies from Caitlyn O'Brien, and mastering by Randy Roos at Squam Sound Studio.
